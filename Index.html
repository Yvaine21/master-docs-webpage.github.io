Cook County Soda Tax been Placed on Hold

Good news or bad news?

Judge Daniel Kubasiak of Cook County Circuit Court issued a temporary restraining order Friday afternoon on the county’s sweetened beverage tax, which was scheduled to go into effect on Saturday.

The penny-per-ounce tax will be applied to all sweetened non-alcoholic beverages sold by retail stores in Cook County, regardless the type of sweetener used.

“I would definitely choose something else, something sugarless for many reasons, for health reasons.” says Zerina Micov, who was putting a case of Coca-Cola into her shopping cart at Aldi, a discount grocery store.

Whether a beverage tax will have health benefits at a societal scale remains uncertain.


Soda drinks Rex Sorgatz/flickr

“People are still probably gonna buy It,” said Sarah Lawrence, a Whole Foods Market customer standing in front of the beverage fridge. “People who are drinking pops don't really care about sugar.”

Ben Paulsen, another customer at Whole Foods Market also expressed his doubt: “You put a tax on the soda, [people] will drink sugary juices instead. Apple juice has like 60 grams of sugar!”

Since the approval of the beverage tax in November 2016, opposing voices have been raised among retailers and consumers. The temporary restraining order was granted as a result of a lawsuit filed by Illinois retailers, according to the Chicago Tribune.

POPS Gozilah52/flickr

“I actually had a customer this afternoon asking about [the tax],” said Alejandra Zaragoza, a floor manager at Aldi. “Customers are complaining, but in terms of [whether they will reduce] consumption, I’m not sure. But probably not.”

President Preckwinkle responded immediately to the suspension of the beverage tax implementation. She wrote in a press statement the Board will appeal the judge’s ruling before the Appellate Court.
